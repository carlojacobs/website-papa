---
titel: The Right Tool on a Sunday
datum: 2026-02-07
samenvatting: A short note about how a calm hour in the shed can fix more than the thing on the bench.
---
There is a particular pleasure in opening an old toolbox and finding exactly the spanner you need on the first try.

It is not really about the spanner. It is about the feeling that the world is, for once, arranged correctly. The screws are in their tin, the light through the window is good, and there is enough time to do the job without rushing it.

I have long suspected that many household problems are made worse by impatience. A wobbly chair becomes a ruined chair because somebody wanted to force it back into place in thirty seconds. A squeaking hinge becomes a broken hinge because nobody stopped to fetch the oil.

Most repairs are improved by the same three things: a rag, a decent cup of coffee, and the willingness to look closely before touching anything.

That is not just advice for sheds and cupboards. It works on people too.
