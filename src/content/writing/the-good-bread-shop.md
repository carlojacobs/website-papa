---
title: The Good Bread Shop
created: 2026-01-19
summary: On the quiet dignity of buying bread from someone who has been awake longer than you have.
---
There is a bakery in town where the bread is still sold by people who seem to take bread personally.

You can see it in the way they wrap a loaf. There is no unnecessary flourish, only the confidence of someone who knows that the product is good and the customer will be back next week.

I admire places like that. They do one thing properly, every day, without trying to turn it into an experience. The experience is simply that the bread is good.

It seems to me that more things ought to be like that. Shoes. Lamps. Letters. Maybe even conversation.

When I came home, I cut two thick slices, put butter on both, and stood at the counter eating in silence. It was an excellent lunch and an even better argument against modern nonsense.
